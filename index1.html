Core Features of AI

Machine Learning

The science of getting computers to act without being explicitly programmed. ML algorithms learn patterns from data.

Neural Networks

Inspired by the human brain, these networks of interconnected nodes are the backbone of deep learning.

Natural Language Processing (NLP)

Enables machines to understand, interpret, and generate human language, powering chatbots and translators.

Computer Vision

Allows computers to see and interpret the visual world, from image recognition to autonomous navigation.

Reinforcement Learning

An agent learns to make decisions by performing actions in an environment to achieve the maximum reward.

Generative AI

AI models that can create new, original content, including text, images, music, and code from input prompts.

Core Features of Artificial Intelligence

Machine Learning

This is AI's most fundamental feature. Instead of being explicitly programmed for every possible scenario, AI systems learn from vast amounts of data. They identify patterns, make predictions, and continuously improve their accuracy over time without human intervention.

Reasoning & Problem-Solving

AI can use logic and rules to analyze situations, make connections, and devise strategies to achieve a specific goal. This allows it to perform tasks like playing complex strategy games, diagnosing technical issues, and optimizing logistics for supply chains.

Perception

This feature allows AI to understand the world through sensory data. Computer Vision lets it "see" and interpret images and videos, while Natural Language Processing (NLP) gives it the ability to understand and respond to human language, both written and spoken.

Action & Interaction

Based on its learning, reasoning, and perception, an AI can take action in the real world. This is most visible in robotics, where AI controls machinery to perform physical tasks, and in autonomous systems like self-driving cars that navigate complex environments.
